THE ALJAFERIA PALACE

About Aljaferia Palace

The Aljaferia Palace is a fortified medieval Islamic palace built during the second half of the 11th century in Zaragoza, Spain. It was originally built as a palace for the Banu Hud dynasty during the Taifa of Zaragoza, but later became the residence of the Christian monarchs after the Reconquista. Today, the palace is one of the most important landmarks of Zaragoza and a popular tourist attraction.

History of Aljaferia Palace

The Aljaferia Palace was built by the Banu Hud dynasty, which ruled Zaragoza during the Taifa period. It was later captured by the Almoravids, who added new structures and decorations to the palace. In the 12th century, the palace was conquered by the Christian Kingdom of Aragon and was used as a residence by the monarchs. Over the years, the palace underwent several renovations and additions, including the construction of the Tower of San Martín in the 14th century.

Visit Aljaferia Palace

The Aljaferia Palace is open to the public and can be visited during certain hours. Admission tickets can be purchased online or at the entrance. Guided tours are also available for an additional fee. The palace is located in the city of Zaragoza, which can be easily reached by train, bus, or car.
