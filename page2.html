THE ALCAZAR OF SEGOVIA

About the Alcazar of Segovia

The Alcazar of Segovia is a medieval fortress located in the old city of Segovia, Spain. Built on a rocky outcrop at the confluence of two rivers, the fortress has a distinctive shape that has been described as resembling the bow of a ship. Over the centuries, the Alcazar has served as a royal palace, a state prison, a military academy, and a museum. Today, it is one of the most popular tourist attractions in Segovia

History of the Alcazar of Segovia

The Alcazar of Segovia was built in the 12th century during the reign of King Alfonso VIII. Over the years, it has been renovated and expanded many times by different monarchs, including King John II and Queen Isabella the Catholic. During the Middle Ages, the fortress was used as a royal palace and served as a backdrop for important events in Spanish history, such as the marriage of Queen Isabella to King Ferdinand II of Aragon. Later, it was used as a state prison and a military academy. In 1862, the Alcazar was damaged by fire and underwent a major restoration that lasted for several decades. Today, it is a museum that showcases the history and architecture of the fortress

Visit the Alcazar of Segovia

The Alcazar of Segovia is open to the public and can be visited during certain hours. Admission tickets can be purchased online or at the entrance. Guided tours are also available for an additional fee. The fortress is located in the old city of Segovia, which can be easily reached by train, bus, or car.
