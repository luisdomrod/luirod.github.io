RUIDERA'S LAGOONS

Visit the Ruidera's Lagoons

The Ruidera Lagoons are a series of interconnected lakes and ponds located in the Campo de Montiel, a region in the province of Ciudad Real in central Spain. The lagoons are situated in the foothills of the Sierra de Alcaraz mountains and are fed by the waters of the Guadiana River. The lagoons are renowned for their crystal-clear waters and stunning natural beauty, which includes waterfalls, caves, cliffs, and lush vegetation. The area is home to a rich diversity of flora and fauna, including various species of fish, amphibians, reptiles, and birds. The Ruidera Lagoons are a popular destination for outdoor activities such as hiking, canoeing, fishing, and swimming. Visitors can also explore the numerous hiking trails that wind through the surrounding countryside, offering breathtaking views of the lagoons and the surrounding landscape. Overall, the Ruidera Lagoons are a true natural wonder, offering visitors an unforgettable experience of the beauty and diversity of the Spanish countryside.

ACTIVITIES

  • Swimming: visitors can swim in some of the lagoons that have been designated for swimming.
  • Kayaking: visitors can rent kayaks to explore the lagoons and enjoy a unique perspective of the scenery.
  • Fishing: visitors can fish in some of the lagoons that are designated for fishing.

Please note that some activities may be seasonal and subject to availability. Visitors should check with the activity providers for up-to-date information and pricing.
