THE CANFRANC INTERNATIONAL RAILWAY STATION

About the canfranc station

Originally opened on july 28,1928, the Canfranc International Railway Station was the largest railway station in Europe at the time. It was built as a symbol of the close relationship between Spain and France, and was a key transportation hub for international travel and trade. Although the station has experienced periods of decline and closure throughout its history, it has undergone extensive restoration efforts in recent years and is now open to visitors. Whether you're interested in the station's history, its stunning architecture, or simply want to take in the beauty of the surrounding Pyrenees Mountains, the Canfranc International Railway Station is an unforgettable destination.

History of The International Railway Station of Canfranc

The Canfranc International Railway Station was built between 1912 and 1928 as a transportation hub between Spain and France. It was the largest railway station in Europe at the time and played a key role in facilitating international travel and trade. During World War II, the station was used to help refugees and resistance fighters escape Nazi-occupied Europe. However, its use declined after the war, and it was eventually closed in 1970 due to a derailment. In recent years, the station has been restored and is now open to visitors. It has been designated a Site of Cultural Interest by the Spanish government and is a popular destination for tourists from around the world.

Activities in the station

Visit The International Railway Station of Canfranc

The Canfranc International Railway Station offers guided tours, providing visitors with a detailed look at the station's history and architecture. You can also explore the station on your own and stop by the station's hotel, which has been restored to its former glory and is now open to visitors.
