"LOS ÓRGANOS, LA GOMERA"

About "Los Órganos"

Los órganos is a geological formation located on the island of La Gomera in the Canary Islands of Spain. It is situated in the Garajonay National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The name "Los Organos" translates to "The Organs" in English, and refers to the tall, vertical basalt columns that resemble organ pipes.

History of "Los Órganos"

The formation of Los Organos dates back millions of years to when the island of La Gomera was formed by volcanic activity. As lava flowed from the volcanoes, it cooled and solidified into basalt rock. Over time, the rock was eroded by wind and water, resulting in the tall, vertical columns that make up Los Organos.

Visit "Los Órganos"

Los Organos is a popular tourist destination and can be reached by hiking trails in the Garajonay National Park. Visitors can explore the formation up close and take in the stunning views of the surrounding landscape. It is important to note that the formation is fragile and visitors are encouraged to take care when exploring the area to avoid damaging the rocks.
